Université de Bordeaux, France
Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.), Computer Science, Bioinformatics, Doctorate (2010 – 2015)

  • Metabolic network is a linked series of biochemical reactions that begins with a particular molecule and converts it into some other molecule or molecules in a carefully defined fashion in living organisms. Studying these networks is one of current challenges in the field of biology, computer science, statistics and related other disciplines. We are working with a technique in graph theory to inhibit the production of a certain objective product in the network. The obtained results are really huge.
  • Classification of these results can be considered as a way to gain in-depth insight into a few of specific functions. For instance, how do we find the solutions to obstruct the production of starch in plant cell metabolism.
  • Finding new methods to model, analyze and predict biological functions in Metabolic Networks.
